Methods

Here are some methods that I use to make my recipes better.

Brining

Brining meat, or soaking it in a salt solution, helps to retain moisture, tenderize the meat, and infuse it with flavor, especially beneficial for lean cuts like chicken and pork. Brining helps prevent lean meats from drying out during cooking by allowing the meat to absorb water, which then gets trapped inside the muscle fibers. Brining also allows flavors from the brine, such as herbs and spices, to penetrate the meat, creating a more flavorful and well-seasoned final product.
How it Works:
The salt in the brine draws water into the meat through osmosis, and the dissolved proteins in the meat form a gel-like structure that traps the water, preventing it from being lost during cooking.
Brining Process:
Soak the meat in the brine solution for a recommended time, typically a few hours to overnight, depending on the size and type of meat.

Marinating

Marinating meat works by adding flavor and aiding texture.

Taste

Layering

Layering ingredients allows some items time to soften or season longer, creating depth of flavor.
